## Design Context

### Users
Domainak serves a mixed audience of technical users, including homelab hobbyists, indie developers, and small teams. They use the product in practical, task-focused contexts where they want to claim a subdomain quickly, connect it to a service, and trust that the control surface will stay understandable as they manage it.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel premium, controlled, and trustworthy. The emotional goal is confidence over hype: users should feel like they are working inside a disciplined operations tool, not a flashy marketing demo.

### Aesthetic Direction
The product should align with a restrained, editorial utility aesthetic inspired by the clarity of Linear, the calm precision of Vercel, and the familiarity of GitHub's product surfaces. Avoid AI-dashboard tropes such as gradient headline text, glassy dark panels, neon accents, generic stat-card grids, and decorative visual effects that do not improve comprehension. Support both light and dark themes with the same token system.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize fast comprehension: users should understand status, actions, and hierarchy at a glance.
2. Prefer restrained utility over decorative flair: visual polish should come from spacing, typography, and clarity, not effects.
3. Use one coherent token system for color, typography, spacing, radii, and motion across all screens.
4. Keep interactions calm and purposeful, with strong focus states and reduced-motion-friendly behavior.
5. Build for mixed technical fluency: concise copy, clear affordances, and progressive disclosure where complexity appears.
